## Introduction

In English, there are different guidelines for capitalizing titles, such as [AP Style](https://www.apstylebook.com/), [Chicago Style](https://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/), [MLA Style](https://www.mla.org/), etc.

To write English articles, we need to adopt a consistent title capitalization style.

As my personal preference, I prefer to use Chicago Style.

## Basic Rules

1. Capitalize the first and last words of the title, regardless of their part of speech.

   Example: "The Great Gatsby" (capitalized "The" and "Gatsby")

2. Capitalize all major words in the title, including nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and subordinating conjunctions.

   Example: "To Kill a Mockingbird" (capitalized "To", "Kill", "Mockingbird")

3. Do not capitalize articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or, for, nor), and prepositions (in, on, at, by, with, etc.) unless they are the first or last word of the title, or they are longer than five letters.

   Example 1: "The Lord of the Rings" (capitalized "The", "Lord", "Rings"; not capitalized "of", "the")

   Example 2: "Work Around the Clock" (capitalized "Work", "Around", "Clock"; not capitalized "the")
