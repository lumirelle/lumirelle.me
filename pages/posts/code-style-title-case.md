## Introduction

In English, there are different guidelines for Title Case, such as [AP Style](https://www.apstylebook.com/), [Chicago Style](https://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/), [MLA Style](https://www.mla.org/), etc.

To write English articles, we need to adopt a consistent Title Case style.

As my personal preference, I uses [Chicago Style](https://title.sh/) (also recommended by [Vercel Web Interface Guidelines](https://vercel.com/design/guidelines)).

## Basic Rules

1. **Capitalize the first and last words** of the title, regardless of their part of speech.

    Example: "To Pass By": Capitalized "To" and "By", because they are the first and last words of the title.

2. **Capitalize all major words** in the title, including nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and subordinating conjunctions.

    Example: "You Are What You Eat": Capitalized "You", "Are", "What", "You", "Eat" because they are major words.

3. **Do not capitalize articles** (a, an, the), **coordinating conjunctions** (and, but, or, for, nor), and **prepositions** (in, on, at, by, with, etc.) unless they are the first or last word of the title, or they are **longer than five letters**.

    Example 1: "Look at the Stars": Not capitalized "at", "the" because they are prepositions and articles.

    Example 2: "All Around the World": Capitalized "Around" because it is an article but longer than five letters.
